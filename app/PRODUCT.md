## Platform

## Users

The person this is built for is a recruiter or hiring manager evaluating Baso Rian Farhan Mallanti as a candidate. They arrive scanning fast, often only semi-technical, judging many people in a short window, and they need to decide quickly whether Rian is worth an interview or a message. Their job to be done is a fast, confident read on his ability and fit. Some reviewers are technical and will open the repos and live links to judge the actual code, so the proof has to hold up to both a skim and a close read. Success is a visitor leaving convinced Rian is a capable, versatile engineer who ships — and taking a next step from browsing the work to reaching out.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Rian, a full-stack and Android developer based in Makassar, Indonesia (currently Fullstack Developer Associate Manager at Carbonethics). It exists to hold an always-on professional presence: a credible, findable calling card that puts 20+ shipped projects in front of the right people and makes Rian easy to evaluate and contact. This is not a lead-generation funnel; it is reputation and reach. Success is a visitor coming away convinced of his range and shipping track record.

## Positioning

One engineer, the full stack. Rian builds real, in-production products end to end across web (MERN, Next.js) and mobile (Android/Kotlin, React Native, Flutter). The single claim every screen reinforces is breadth backed by shipped proof — not a list of technologies, but evidence that he takes things all the way to something people use.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: browse the projects — let the work do the convincing. Secondary CTA (for visitors not ready to dig in): contact / hire me, via the existing EmailJS contact page.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: *20+ apps shipped end-to-end — Android, MERN, and Next.js.*
- Belief ladder: first, this is a real, serious engineer and not a recycled template; then, he has genuine full-stack range across web and mobile; then, the work actually ships and gets used — live links and real clients, not toy demos; finally, he would be a low-risk, capable hire worth contacting.
- Proof on hand: employer and client names as credibility markers (Carbonethics, Disnaker Makassar, SMKN 4 Jeneponto, Hacktiv8); testimonials and recommendations to gather and feature; 20 live projects with public GitHub repositories; and a Certificates page backing the stated skills.

## Brand Personality

Precise, technical, confident. The voice is an engineer's — direct, specific, proof-first, no marketing fluff. The impression to leave in the first seconds is *this person clearly knows their craft*: sharp and technically impressive ahead of warm or playful. The site's existing world — terminal, scan lines, corner brackets, monospace, the interactive `neofetch` About section — fits this personality and is worth keeping as identity rather than replacing.

## Anti-references

Not the generic one-page developer template thousands of people ship — the interchangeable, forgettable portfolio is the thing to beat. Not over-designed or noisy: effects and animation must never bury the work or hurt readability. And not the instantly-recognizable AI-generated look — decorative gradients, glassmorphism-by-default, and cross-project sameness that make a visitor think "an AI made this."

## Design Principles

Show the work, don't claim it. Proof beats adjectives; let the shipped projects and live links carry the argument, which is why browsing the work is the primary action.

Engineer's rigor, made visible. Precision and technical confidence should read in the craft of the details themselves — the medium is part of the message.

Distinct, never templated. Reject the interchangeable dev-portfolio and the AI-generated aesthetic; specificity to Rian is the whole differentiator.

Range is the story. One engineer, full stack, web to mobile — make the breadth legible without turning it into noise.

Legible under motion. The experience is deliberately animated and always-on, so readability, hierarchy, and contrast must survive the motion rather than compete with it. This is the line that keeps "always-on animation" from becoming "over-designed and noisy."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target has been set. There is a deliberate product decision to keep motion always on: animations run regardless of the operating system's reduced-motion preference (see `ThemeContext.jsx` and the About terminal, where reduced-motion is intentionally ignored). Because motion is a constant, the accessibility weight shifts onto what stays fixed — strong text contrast, clear hierarchy, and never gating content visibility on an animation, so text is readable even mid-reveal. Keep motion smooth and avoid extreme vestibular triggers even while it always plays.
